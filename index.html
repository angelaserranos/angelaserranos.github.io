Environmental Research on Persistent Pollutants

IES Arquitecto Ventura Rodríguez — High School Research Project

This project evaluated the presence of polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDEs) and polychlorinated dibenzo-p-dioxins and dibenzofurans (PCDD/Fs) in Spanish sewage sludge. These compounds are classified as Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs), chemical substances that resist environmental degradation, accumulate through food chains, and are widely embedded in everyday products such as industrial flame retardants or household goods.

Data from different autonomous communities was evaluated to study the effect of different treatment methods in the pollutant levels. The use of the Shapiro-Wilk text revealed that the analysed samples did not adjust to normal distributions. Therefore, non-parametrical methods were used.

The U-Mann-Whitney test was used to find statistical differences between Wastewater Treatment Plants (WWTPs) usign anaerobic and aerobic digesters.

Results concluded that the use and production of these compounds has kept stable during the last years. No significant differences (neither increase nor decrease) in the concentrations were found based on the treatment method, suggesting that neither PBDEs nor PCDD/Fs are susceptible to incrase or decrease their presence by the use of anaerobic or aerobic digesters.

This project was my first contact with academy, requiring me to search for useful research papers related to the topic and figure out how to apply the different statistical methods to my data. Although it was a bit confusing at the beginning, it was a very enriching experience to increase my knowledge about a new topic and improve my problem-solving skills.
